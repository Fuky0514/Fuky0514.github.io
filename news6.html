Official: Starch Sausage Report Fake
Image source: Reveal News / Stock

Recently, a "research report" titled "Shocking! Over 70% of Hot Dogs on the Market Have Fake Ingredients, XX Consumers' Association Issues a Solemn Warning" has gone viral on multiple social media platforms and some communities. The document mimics the format and seal of official documents and is accompanied by "laboratory test data" and "expert interpretations" of unknown origin, claiming that through "large-scale random inspections", it was found that hot dog products generally have serious problems such as the illegal use of additives and passing off inferior goods as good ones, which has caused concern among some consumers and directly affected the market sales of related products.

As the impact of the incident expanded, the "XX City Consumers' Association" mentioned under a false name and its superior administrative unit responded promptly. Today, the city's Market Supervision and Administration Bureau, in conjunction with the Consumers' Association, issued an official "Statement and Notice". The notice clearly stated that the so-called "investigation report" was purely fabricated, and the so-called "random inspection", "data" and "conclusion" mentioned in it had no factual basis at all. The names of the institutions, the style of the official seals and the document numbers used in the document were all forged. Relevant departments emphasized that they had never authorized or released any investigation report or risk warning regarding "widespread fraud in the hot dog industry".

Preliminary online traceability information shows that this forged report first appeared on a personal self-media account that mainly posts eye-catching content. It was then forwarded by multiple marketing accounts and netizens who were unaware of the truth. The nodes of its spread clearly exhibit the feature of "creating panic to attract traffic". The market supervision department stated that it is currently investigating the source of the forged official document and the spread of rumors in accordance with the law.

Legal experts in the field of food safety have analyzed the dangers of false news about people's livelihood. They point out that such precisely fabricated rumors that imitate official sources and target common food products are extremely harmful. Firstly, they directly infringe upon the legitimate rights and interests of regular food production and operation enterprises, causing them to suffer unjustified damage to their reputation and economic losses. Secondly, they unreasonably trigger public panic over the safety of specific food categories, disrupting the normal market order and consumer confidence. Lastly, they seriously deplete and damage the credibility of official authoritative release channels, significantly reducing the effectiveness of future genuine risk warnings.

Regulatory authorities remind consumers to obtain food safety information from official websites and government WeChat accounts of market supervision and administration departments, health and wellness departments, and consumer associations at all levels. For various "internal reports" or "urgent notices" circulating on the Internet, consumers should verify the authenticity of the issuing unit and the standardization of the document format. They can also proactively verify through the official phone numbers or platforms announced to avoid gullibility, blind following, and spreading.

At present, relevant online platforms have begun to investigate and handle the discovered rumor links. This incident once again shows that in the information age, the ability to distinguish the authenticity of information is an important skill for every consumer to protect their own rights and interests and maintain a healthy market environment. For the malicious fabrication and dissemination of false food safety information, the law will surely impose severe punishment.
