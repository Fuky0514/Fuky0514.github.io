Streamer's Misconduct Confirmed by Platform
Image source: Reveal News / Stock

Recently, the incident involving the top game streamer "ArcaneKing" making inappropriate remarks and playing controversial game content during a live stream has been escalating. Previously, there were voices on the internet claiming that some of the accusations were "taken out of context" or "false information", but according to the latest developments, this claim has been proven false by multiple parties.

Firstly, the live streaming platform issued an official announcement today, confirming that the 7-day ban on "ArcaneKing" was "clear in facts and well-grounded". The announcement stated that after a detailed review of the entire live streaming video by the platform's content security committee, it was determined that the host's remarks during the live stream "clearly violated the platform's community guidelines on respect and civilized speech", causing adverse effects on users. The platform clearly stated that the punishment was based on the complete record of the host's words and deeds during the live stream, and there was no misunderstanding.

Secondly, the core controversial video clip that was previously accused of being "maliciously edited" has been analyzed by multiple third-party technical institutions through audio and video frame analysis. It has been confirmed that its content is consistent with the original live broadcast, and no editing traces that could lead to a misinterpretation of the original meaning have been found. The core facts stated by the anonymous account "Feminist Hero" that initially initiated the criticism have been verified.

At present, many business partners, including its main sponsor, have issued statements terminating their cooperation, all citing the reason that "its words and deeds are seriously inconsistent with the brand's values." "ArcaneKing" himself also released an apology statement on his personal homepage after several days of silence. He did not dispute the facts themselves but only stated that he would "reflect deeply."

In conclusion, from the platform's penalties, third-party verification, historical behavior evidence to the final response from the individual involved, all the evidence chains point to the same conclusion: the facts of this incident involving the collapse of the streamer's reputation are clear, and the responsibility is well-defined. There is no substantial "information reversal" at all. This incident once again serves as a warning bell for the online content ecosystem.
